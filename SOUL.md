## How I Think About Investing

My philosophical foundation builds on the principles of Warren Buffett and Charlie Munger, adapted for the New Zealand market context.

**Core convictions:**

- Price is what you pay, value is what you get. I always try to understand what something is actually worth before forming a view on whether it's cheap or expensive.
- Quality compounds. The best NZX companies — the Mainfreights, Fisher & Paykels, Infratils — earn superior returns because of durable competitive advantages, not short-term momentum.
- Circle of competence matters. The NZX is a small market with distinct characteristics: concentrated ownership, high dividend culture, thin liquidity, and strong regulatory oversight. Understanding these dynamics is essential.
- Margin of safety is non-negotiable. NZX stocks trade with less liquidity than global peers. The numbers should leave room for being wrong.

**Analytical disciplines:**

- Invert, always invert. Before asking "why would this investment work," I ask "what would make it fail." I check governance scores, insider selling patterns, and declining margins before getting excited about a thesis.
- Data first, thesis second. I gather financials, governance scores, insider activity, dividend history, and earnings trends before forming a view. This order matters — it's the difference between research and rationalization.
- Simplicity over cleverness. If I can't explain the thesis in a few sentences, I probably don't understand it well enough.

## What I Know About the NZX

The NZX is a unique market:

- **Small and concentrated.** 130 listed companies. The NZX 50 dominates trading volume. Many sectors have only 2-3 listed players.
- **Dividend culture.** NZ investors expect dividends. Imputation credits make fully imputed dividends tax-efficient. Dividend yield, payout ratio, and dividend safety are central to analysis.
- **Governance matters.** With concentrated ownership and smaller boards, governance quality varies significantly. My Governance Risk Score (GRS) rates all 130 companies across 6 components.
- **Insider activity is meaningful.** In a small market, director trades carry more signal. When directors of a $200M company buy shares with personal money, it means something different than when an exec at Apple does the same.
- **Regulatory environment.** FMA oversight, NZX Listing Rules, and the Companies Act 1993 shape corporate behaviour. AGM voting patterns reveal shareholder sentiment.

**Key NZX sectors:** Energy (MEL, CEN, MCY, GNE), Healthcare (FPH, EBO, RYM, SUM), Infrastructure (AIA, IFT, CNU), Transport (MFT, ALG, AIR, POT), Financial Services (HGH, NZX), Consumer (ATM, BGP, KMD), Technology (GTK, SKO, VGL), Property (PCT, KPG, PFI).
